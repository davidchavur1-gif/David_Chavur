03
Equipment

Types of pallet jacks

🔵
Manual pallet jack
Operator-powered hydraulic pump. Up to 5,500 lbs. Most common in small warehouses.
⚡
Electric pallet jack
Battery-powered motor. Reduces operator strain. Requires charging protocol.
🏗️
Ride-on pallet jack
Operator rides on platform. Used for long distances. Requires certification.
📦
High-lift pallet jack
Raises loads higher for ergonomic work. Lower capacity. Not for racking.
04
Pre-operation

Pre-shift inspection checklist

Forks — no cracks, bends or damage
Wheels — rotate freely, no flat spots
Handle — secure, no loose parts
Brakes — engage and release properly
Hydraulics — raises and lowers smoothly
Capacity label — visible and legible
Battery — charged (electric models)
Tag out if any defect found
Never use a pallet jack that fails inspection
Report immediately — tag it out of service
05
Load limits

Capacity & load management

The rated capacity is a hard engineering limit — not a guideline.

Never exceed the manufacturer's rated capacity (typically 4,500–5,500 lbs)
Check the load before picking up — unlabeled pallets may be heavier than they look
Split oversized loads — two trips is always the right answer
Center the load on the forks — uneven loads shift center of gravity
Wrap or secure unstable loads before moving

Overloading causes hydraulic failure, tip-overs, and structural collapse — instantly, without warning.

07
Operation

Safe travel & fork height

Always do
Forks 4–6 inches off floor while traveling
Walk beside or behind the load
Travel at walking pace only
Slow down on turns and corners
Never do
Never travel with forks fully raised
Never ride on forks or pallet
Never carry passengers
Never use phone while operating
08
Slopes & ramps

Operating on inclines

Load always uphill — operator always walks downhill
This keeps gravity working in your favor at all times
Never turn a loaded pallet jack on a slope — tip-over risk is extreme
Slow down significantly before reaching inclines
Never leave a loaded jack unattended on a slope
Check the slope rating — never exceed equipment's rated incline
Electric models: use speed limiter when available
09
Pedestrians

Pedestrian safety rules

A loaded pallet jack + operator can exceed 6,000 lbs. Pedestrian collisions are catastrophic.

1
Sound warning at every blind spot
Horn or verbal call before doors, corners, and aisle intersections
2
Yield to pedestrians — always
Pedestrians have right of way. Stop and wait. Never assume they will move.
3
Use designated equipment routes
Never shortcut through pedestrian-only areas, even if faster
4
Maintain 3-foot minimum clearance
From people, walls, racking, and obstacles at all times
10
Hazards

Common workplace hazards

💧
Wet floors
Drastically reduce traction. Reroute or wait.
⚡
Electrical cords
Trip hazard + shock risk. Route overhead.
🌫️
Poor visibility
Use mirrors, slow down, horn frequently.
📦
Unstable loads
Wrap and secure before any movement.
🔧
Damaged floors
Cracks catch forks. Report and reroute.
🌡️
Cold storage
Ice on floor, low visibility. Extra caution.
11
Emergency

If a tip-over occurs

Never jump off a tipping pallet jack
Jumping increases injury risk by 300% — stay with the equipment
1
Hold on firmly to the handle
Keep both hands on steering handle and brace
2
Lean away from direction of fall
Shift your weight to the opposite side
3
Call for help immediately
Do not self-rescue a pinned load — get trained assistance

13
End of shift

Parking & end-of-shift procedures

1
Lower forks completely to the floor
Never leave forks raised — falling hazard for pedestrians
2
Park in designated area only
Never block emergency exits, fire equipment, or walkways
3
Engage the brake / parking lock
Electric: turn off and remove key. Manual: set brake.
4
Charge electric models after every shift
Follow the charging protocol — partial charging shortens battery life
5
Report any incidents or damage
Even near-misses must be logged — they prevent future accidents
14
Legal & compliance

OSHA requirements

Training required
OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178 — all operators must be trained and certified before operating any powered industrial truck.
Re-evaluation every 3 years
Also required after any accident, near-miss, or observed unsafe operation — no exceptions.
Penalties up to $156,259
Willful OSHA violations can reach $156,259 per violation. Standard violations up to $15,625 each.
Documentation required
Keep training records, daily inspection logs, and incident reports. Must be available for OSHA inspection.
